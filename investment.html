Industry & Investment

The Department of Industry and Investment is a critical arm of the Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment (MTII), responsible for driving the industrial and investment growth of the state. Its role extends beyond just facilitating industrial development and investment opportunities; it serves as a catalyst for economic transformation by creating an enabling environment for both local and foreign investors, promoting industrial policies, and ensuring the sustainable development of industries within the economy.

In a rapidly globalizing economy, the Department plays an instrumental role in aligning national industrial policies with international best practices, ensuring that industries not only thrive but also remain competitive in the global market. The Department works closely with other governmental bodies, private sector players, and international organizations to unlock the full potential of the country’s industrial and investment landscape.

Vision

The Department of Industry and Investment aspires to be a leading force in the economic transformation of the nation by promoting industrialization and attracting sustainable investments that contribute to job creation, economic diversification, and global competitiveness. Through the development of an inclusive and competitive industrial sector, the Department aims to enhance the country’s industrial output and position it as a key player in the global economy.

Mission

The Department’s mission is to foster an enabling environment for industrial growth and investment, support the development of a robust industrial base, and encourage the diversification of the economy through targeted investments in key sectors. This is achieved by providing policy direction, developing infrastructure, facilitating investment, and fostering industrial innovation and sustainable practices.

Key Functions of the Department

  • Promotion of Industrial Growth: Developing and implementing strategies for industrial expansion, modernization, and diversification. Encouraging the establishment of new industries in key sectors such as manufacturing, technology, agriculture, and energy. Supporting the growth of Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs) through grants, funding schemes, and infrastructure support.
  • Investment Attraction and Facilitation: Promoting the country as an attractive investment destination by developing investment policies and incentives for both local and international investors. Coordinating investment initiatives, such as investment summits, trade missions, and investor roadshows. Assisting investors with the necessary regulatory approvals, permits, and licenses to ensure smooth establishment and operation of investments. Providing a platform for public-private partnerships (PPPs) aimed at large-scale industrial projects.
  • Policy Development and Industrial Regulation: Formulating policies to drive industrial development while ensuring compliance with global trade standards. Conducting regular reviews of industrial policies to meet the evolving needs of industries and the investment climate. Working with other agencies to ensure that industries comply with local and international regulations, including environmental standards, labor laws, and safety guidelines.
  • Industrial Capacity Building: Organizing training and capacity-building programs to upgrade skills in the industrial sector, especially for entrepreneurs and workforce development. Supporting research and development (R&D) to foster innovation and increase the competitiveness of industries. Encouraging the adoption of modern technologies and best practices across industries to improve productivity and efficiency.
  • Industrial Infrastructure Development: Facilitating the creation of industrial parks, special economic zones (SEZs), and industrial clusters to boost manufacturing and industrial output. Developing and improving critical infrastructure, including power, transport, and logistics systems, to support industrial activities. Coordinating with local governments and private sector partners to ensure the availability of industrial land and necessary utilities.
  • Export Promotion and International Trade Relations: Supporting the export sector by facilitating trade agreements, removing trade barriers, and promoting Made-in-Nigeria goods. Building and strengthening trade relations with other countries, creating new markets for locally produced goods and services. Monitoring global market trends to provide timely information and opportunities to local industries.
  • Sustainability and Environmental Impact: Promoting sustainable industrial practices by encouraging the use of clean energy, waste reduction, and environmentally friendly technologies. Collaborating with environmental agencies to ensure industries meet environmental compliance standards. Supporting industries in their transition to greener technologies and practices that reduce their carbon footprint.
  • Investment Monitoring and Evaluation: Monitoring the performance of both local and foreign investments to ensure that they are meeting agreed-upon terms, including economic impact, job creation, and sustainability. Providing continuous support to investors through aftercare services, ensuring that their investments grow successfully within the national framework. Evaluating the effectiveness of investment policies and industrial programs, ensuring that they are delivering tangible benefits to the economy.
