Registration Fees

Registration fees are mandatory charges required to formalize and legally recognize various types of organizations, businesses, or entities. These fees cover administrative processing, certification, and compliance with regulatory frameworks established by the governing authorities. The amount of registration fees typically varies based on the type and size of the entity, such as cooperative societies, community-based organizations (CBOs), or faith-based organizations (FBOs). For businesses, registration fees often depend on the nature of the enterprise, its legal structure (e.g., sole proprietorship, partnership, or corporation), and the jurisdiction where it operates. Payment of these fees ensures the entity's legal status, enabling it to operate lawfully and access relevant benefits, such as tax identification numbers, government support, or legal protections.

Registration Zones

The registration process is organized into three (3) zones to ensure efficient coverage based on geographic and administrative divisions. Please select the button corresponding to the zone that includes your location.

Zone A: Urban and Suburban Centers

Zone A encompasses major urban and suburban areas, serving as the administrative and economic hubs of the state. These locations are characterized by higher population density, more developed infrastructure, and proximity to the state capital. This zone includes:

  • Lafia: The state capital and central administrative hub.
  • Mararaba Gurku: A fast-growing suburban area near the Federal Capital Territory.
  • One Man Village: A densely populated residential area.
  • Nyanya Gwandara: A suburban community known for its proximity to urban centers.
  • Masaka: A commercial town with significant residential development.
  • Keffi: An academic and cultural hub, hosting notable institutions and historical sites.

Zone B: Local Government Headquarters

Zone B includes the 13 Local Government Headquarters, excluding the towns in Zone A. These locations function as administrative centers for their respective local government areas, providing governance, commerce, and services for surrounding communities. The towns in this zone are:

  • Akwanga
  • Awe
  • Doma
  • Karu
  • Keana
  • Kokona
  • Nasarawa
  • Nasarawa Eggon
  • Obi
  • Toto
  • Wamba
Click to See Registration Fee

Zone C: Rural and Underserved Areas

Zone C comprises all rural areas in the state that are not mentioned in Zones A or B. These locations are typically less populated, with limited infrastructure and resources. The focus in this zone is on ensuring inclusivity and reaching underserved communities. This zone covers remote villages, agricultural settlements, and other rural areas across the state.
